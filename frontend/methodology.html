Technical Methodology

How AquaSentinel AI processes satellite data and AI imagery to protect our oceans.

1. Data Acquisition

We integrate directly with NASA's Earthdata ecosystem using the Common Metadata Repository (CMR) to discover relevant oceanographic datasets. GIBS (Global Imagery Browse Services) is used to provide high-resolution satellite imagery overlays for visual monitoring.

  • MODIS Aqua/Terra Chlorophyll-a
  • VIIRS Sea Surface Temperature
  • Copernicus Marine Wave Analysis

2. AI Prediction Pipeline

Our system uses a dual-module AI architecture for comprehensive marine analysis:

Debris Detection

A YOLOv8-based object detection model trained on marine debris datasets to identify plastic, nets, and waste.

Ecosystem Segmentation

U-Net architecture for semantic segmentation of water, coral, algae, and turbid zones.

3. Marine Risk Engine

The Risk Engine synthesizes data from all sources to calculate a localized Risk Level (LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH, CRITICAL). The formula considers:

Debris Density Impact: 40%
Chlorophyll/Algae Impact: 25%
Wave Dynamics Impact: 20%
Zone Proximity Impact: 15%
